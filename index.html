Colossal Return of the Wooly Mammoths

The is my Peer Reviewed Research Argument Essay, in which I argue that wooly mammoths, a species that went extinct over 4000 years ago, should be brought back to life. I talked about different consequences of bringing them back, good and the bad, and why the good outweighs the bad. I've addressed my essay to four scientists and other scientists who are similarly skeptical of this project: Love Dal√©n, professor of evolutionary genetics at the Swedish Museum of Natural History, Joseph Frederickson, a vertebrate paleontologist and director of the Weis Earth Science Museum in Menasha, Paul Knoepfler, a professor at UC Davis School of Medicine in Cell Biology and Human Anatomy, Matthew Cobb, a professor of zoology at the University of Manchester, Steve Brusatte, an American paleontologist and evolutionary biologist who specializes in the anatomy and evolution of dinosaurs. I picked these scientists because they have criticized the Colossal for resurrection of the mammoths and argue against bringing back the wooly mammoths. They believe it is better to save the current species rather than bringing the old back. Through peer reviewed research and other relevant sources, I have attempted to persuade these scientists to support resurrection of the wooly mammoths, instead of going against it. I talked about how we have enough capital and resources to save the current species and bring them back.

Beyond The Walls

This is my second essay. It is a personal narrative about my own sense of belonging at Stony Brook University. I focus on my experiences of spending eighteen years of my life in India, and how I felt like I never belonged there. I realized how I have more in common with the people here in United States than people back in India. My life here at Stony Brook University is filled with people who are just like me. I constructed a narrative with telling details to co nvey that people shouldn't limit themselves by their own country's imaginary walls, and try to see outside these walls, because they might find a place they feel like they belong to, like I did.

ReLinked

ReLinked is a simple flask based application which allows the user to save important webpages. You have the ability to name those links, search through the links as well as create link collections ( called ReNodes ) which allows the user to group links together for easier access. You can also choose an icon to display next to your saved link from 1816 available icon choices! Lastly, the webpage has an entire darkmode which can be toggled from the navigation bar. Your dark/light mode preference is stored on-device using cookies so that you don't have to worry about re-enabling it. There is also a guide section in the webpage which guides the user on how to use the application.
